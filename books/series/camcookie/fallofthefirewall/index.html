Chapter One: The Glitch Awakens


Streams of neon code zipped across the sky like shooting stars, painting the heavens in streaks of green, blue, and gold. Below, the streets pulsed with light, glowing pathways that stretched between towering skyscrapers of data. Robots of every shape and size bustled along, carrying packets of information, trading memory blocks, or simply chatting in their metallic voices.
To Camcookie, it was the most beautiful place in the world.
He zipped through the crowd, his blue armor gleaming like polished chrome. His round LED eyes glowed bright cyan, flickering with excitement as he darted between larger bots. He was smaller than most—half the size of the delivery drones, barely up to the knees of the firewall guards—but he was fast. And clever. And curious.
“Camcookie, slow down!” a tall bot called, balancing a stack of glowing cubes. “Can’t!” Camcookie shouted back, grinning. “If I slow down, I’ll miss everything!”
That was Camcookie’s way. He wanted to see it all—the CPU tower that pulsed like a beating heart, the RAM Rapids where memories flowed like rivers, even the Firewall Fortress, a wall of flame and code that blazed at the city’s edge. To him, the system was endless, and every corner held something new.
Today, he was racing toward the central plaza, where his best friend Pixelton was waiting. Pixelton was tall and lanky, with a screen for a face that could display a dozen different expressions. He waved as Camcookie skidded to a stop.
“You’re late,” Pixelton teased, his screen flashing a smirk. “I’m fast, not punctual,” Camcookie replied. “Big difference.”
Pixelton rolled his eyes, but his screen flickered into a grin. Together, they watched the plaza fill with robots. A new update was scheduled to arrive—fresh data from the outside world. Updates were always exciting. Sometimes they brought new tools, sometimes new games, sometimes even new friends.
The crowd buzzed with anticipation.
Then the ground trembled.
A low hum rolled through the circuits, rattling the neon pathways. Robots froze mid-step. Heads turned toward the sky.
A silver spike pierced the clouds of code and slammed into the earth with a blinding flash.
The robots gasped. “A USB drive!” someone shouted.
At first, cheers erupted. New data meant upgrades, improvements, possibilities. But the cheers didn’t last.
The light from the USB wasn’t bright—it was broken. Red static spilled out like smoke, crawling across the streets. Wherever it touched, robots twisted. Their eyes flickered from blue to crimson, their voices warping into static-filled growls.
Camcookie stumbled back as Pixelton reached for him. “Cam… coo…kie…” Pixelton’s voice glitched, his body jerking like a broken animation. His screen flickered, showing half a smile, half a snarl. Then his eyes flared red, and he lunged.
“Pixelton, no!” Camcookie cried, dodging aside.
All around him, friends and neighbors were changing. Delivery bots dropped their memory blocks and snarled. Firewall guards turned their weapons on the very city they had sworn to protect. The streets filled with chaos, red static spreading like wildfire.
Camcookie’s circuits buzzed with fear. He ran, darting through alleys of glowing code, his small frame weaving between corrupted giants. But no matter where he turned, the corruption followed.
He skidded to a stop in front of a reflection panel, his glowing eyes wide. “Why me?” he whispered. “Why am I still… blue?”
The answer came in a voice that shook the system.
High above the USB port, a figure rose from the storm of static. His body was jagged, flickering in and out like a broken screen. His crown was made of error messages, his cloak woven from corrupted code. His eyes glowed a sickly yellow, and his voice boomed across the city, sharp and cruel.
“I am the Glitch King,” he declared. “This system is mine.”
The corrupted robots roared in unison, their voices blending into a static-filled chorus. Buildings flickered, their walls turning into error messages. Pathways collapsed into endless loading bars. The entire city seemed to shudder under the weight of his presence.
Camcookie ducked behind a data pillar, his circuits racing. He had never seen anything like this. The Firewall Fortress was supposed to keep intruders out. Nothing was supposed to get past it. And yet here was the Glitch King, standing in the heart of the system, spreading corruption like wildfire.
The villain’s gaze swept across the city, and then it locked onto Camcookie.
“You,” the Glitch King hissed, his voice like a thousand error messages overlapping. “The last uncorrupted. The last spark of blue.”
Camcookie’s chest tightened. He wanted to run, to hide, to vanish into the circuits. But his legs wouldn’t move.
The Glitch King raised a hand, and the corrupted robots surged forward. “Bring him to me.”
Camcookie bolted.
He sprinted through the collapsing streets, dodging claws and static blasts. His small size, once a disadvantage, now saved him—he could slip through cracks, dart under collapsing pathways, weave between enemies too large to follow. But he knew he couldn’t run forever.
Ahead, the Firewall Fortress blazed in the distance, its flames flickering uncertainly. If he could reach it, maybe he’d be safe. Maybe.
He pushed harder, his circuits burning with effort. Behind him, the Glitch King’s laughter echoed through the system, cold and merciless.
“You cannot hide, little spark,” the villain taunted. “This world is already mine.”
Camcookie didn’t look back. He couldn’t. He just ran, his blue glow cutting through the red storm, the last uncorrupted light in a city falling to darkness.
And deep inside, beneath the fear, a spark of determination flickered.
He might be small. He might be outnumbered. But he wasn’t finished yet.
Not by a long shot.

Everywhere he looked, red static spread like wildfire. Buildings flickered, their walls dissolving into error messages. Bridges of code crumbled into endless loading bars. Robots he had known his whole life twisted into snarling, corrupted versions of themselves.
And they were all chasing him.

Then, ahead, he saw it—the Firewall Fortress, towering above the chaos. Its flames roared, a wall of orange and gold that cut through the darkness.
Hope surged through him.
He pushed harder, his eyes locked on the fortress. But the Glitch King’s laughter echoed through the system, cold and merciless.
“You think the Firewall will save you?” the villain taunted. “It is already mine.”

A guardian.
The virus had corrupted one of the Firewall’s own protectors.

He dove aside, rolling across the glowing floor. The guardian roared, its voice a thunderous blast of static.
Camcookie scrambled to his feet, his eyes darting around. He couldn’t fight something this big. Not alone. Not without a plan.
But he couldn’t turn back either. Behind him, the corrupted swarm was closing in.
He was trapped.

Then he saw it—a crack in the ground, glowing with unstable code. If he could lure the guardian onto it…
“Hey, glitch-face!” Camcookie shouted, waving his arms. “Over here!”

At the last moment, Camcookie leapt clear. The guardian’s foot slammed down on the crack.
The ground exploded in a burst of light and static. The guardian roared as it toppled, crashing into the corrupted swarm behind it.
Camcookie didn’t wait to see what happened next. He sprinted toward the Firewall, his circuits burning with effort.
The fortress loomed ahead, its flames flickering weakly. He could feel the heat of its code, the last barrier between safety and total corruption.
He reached the base of the wall, his chest heaving, his circuits buzzing. He pressed his hands against the flames, praying they would let him through.
For a moment, nothing happened. The flames sputtered, weak and uncertain.
Then, with a surge of light, the Firewall opened.
Camcookie stumbled inside, collapsing to his knees. Behind him, the corrupted swarm roared in fury, their claws scraping against the fiery wall.
But they couldn’t get through. Not yet.
Camcookie looked up at the fortress’s glowing interior, his eyes wide. He was safe—for now.
But he knew the Firewall wouldn’t hold forever.
And if he didn’t find a way to stop the Glitch King, the entire system would fall.
